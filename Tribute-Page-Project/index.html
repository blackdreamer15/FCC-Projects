Dorothy Vaughan

The Human Computer

Here is the life timeline of Dorothy Vaughan:

  • 1910 - was born on September 20 in Kansas City, MO
  • 1929 - graduated from the college Wilberforce University
  • 1932 - married Howard Vaughan
  • 1943 - began work for NACA at the Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia
  • 1958 - served as the head of the West Net Computers until NACA became known NASA
  • 1961 - moved into the area of electronic computing in 1961, after NACA introduced the first digital (non-human) computers to the center. Vaughan became proficient in computer programming, teaching herself FORTRAN and teaching it to her coworkers to prepare them for the transition. She contributed to the space program through her work on the Scout Launch Vehicle Program
  • 1971 - retired from NASA at the age of 61 after working at NASA-Langley for 28 years
  • 2008 - died on November 10 aged 98
